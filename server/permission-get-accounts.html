GET_ACCOUNTS Permission Policy

How Clickeros AI uses the GET_ACCOUNTS permission — purpose, data handling, and your controls.

Effective: May 11, 2026 Clickeros AI, Inc.

Permission Overview

👤
GET_ACCOUNTS
android.permission.GET_ACCOUNTS
Permission TypeNormal on Android 8.0+ (auto-granted); Dangerous on older versions
Required?Optional — only needed if you choose to sign in with Google or another account provider
When RequestedOnly when you tap "Sign in with Google" on the login or sign-up screen
Background AccessNever — account list is only read during the sign-in flow you initiate

Why We Need This Permission

Use CaseWhat We AccessWhat We Do NOT Access
Google Sign-In (OAuth)The list of accounts on your device so you can select one to sign in withPasswords, tokens, email content, contacts, or any data beyond the email and name you authorize
Email Pre-fillYour selected account email to pre-fill account formsAny other account information
Session MaintenanceYour account email to maintain your login sessionAccount credentials or authentication tokens beyond the OAuth token you authorize
We only access the account you explicitly select. The account picker dialog is controlled by Android/Google, not by our app.

Data Handling

AspectDetails
Data CollectedEmail address and display name of the account you select and authorize via Google Sign-In
TransmissionEncrypted via TLS 1.3. OAuth token exchange handled by Google secure OAuth 2.0 flow.
StorageYour email address stored in our secure database to identify your account
Shared WithNot sold or shared for marketing. Shared with Google only as part of the OAuth flow you initiate.
RetentionStored until you delete your account. Cleared from local device storage on logout.
EncryptionAES-256 at rest; TLS 1.3 in transit
Alternative: You can sign in with email and password instead, which does not require the GET_ACCOUNTS permission. Tap "Sign in with Email" on the login screen.
No background access. This permission is only activated during an explicit action you initiate within the app.
To remove Google account access: Visit myaccount.google.com/permissions, find Clickeros AI, and click "Remove Access."

How to Revoke This Permission

Android: Settings → Apps → Clickeros AI → Permissions → Accounts → Deny.

iOS: This permission is not required on iOS. Google Sign-In on iOS uses a different mechanism that does not require device account access.
